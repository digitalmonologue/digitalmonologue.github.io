Why Sharing History is Important

分享家族病史：给长辈的核心要点

1. Find problems early (及早发现)

Knowing your history helps doctors decide when to start checks. If relatives had colorectal cancer, you may need to start screening at 40 instead of 50. If a mother or sister had breast cancer, you are at a higher risk and should get mammograms more frequently.

2. Save your life (挽救生命)

Diseases like cancer are much more treatable when caught early. For instance, the 5-year survival rate for early-stage colorectal cancer is over 90%. Knowing your risk is the first step to early detection.

3. Not just cancer (不只是癌症)

Family history is also a key factor for other common conditions, such as heart disease and stroke.

4. You can take action (积极行动)

Once you know your history, you can work with your doctor on a plan. This might include regular check-ups (using free programs) and healthy habits like eating well and avoiding smoking.
